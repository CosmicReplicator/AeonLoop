Explanation in Plain Words

Imagine the universe has a built-in clock that ticks incredibly quickly---at a frequency of 1.667 THz, which means there are 1.667 trillion ticks every second. If you reverse that frequency, you get an interval of about 0.6 picoseconds per tick. This 0.6 ps is the tiny time window during which the state of the universe is updated, serving as a basic unit of time in our picture.\ \ During one of these ultra-short ticks, even light---the fastest thing in the universe---travels only about 0.18 millimeters. This distance sets a natural spatial scale: it represents the tiny region in which significant quantum events or updates can occur in that brief moment.\ \ Now, in quantum mechanics, every process accumulates phase over time. By insisting that each tick completes a full cycle (meaning the phase returns to its starting point), we define a fundamental energy step. After taking into account that the effective geometry of space is \'compressed\' from 3D to an effective 2.667 dimensions, that energy step becomes about 5 millielectronvolts. In our model, this value also matches the measured mass of the neutrino, suggesting that the neutrino represents the minimal quantum update---the smallest energy packet that nature deals with.\ \ In summary, the 1.667 THz frequency defines an ultra-short tick of about 0.6 ps. In that brief period, light covers only 0.18 mm, and the rules of quantum mechanics then require a fundamental energy step of around 5 meV. These numbers are all interrelated, forming the basis of a framework that unifies time, space, and energy into a single consistent picture.
