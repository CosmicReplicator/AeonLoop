7 · Emergent Gravitational & Strong-Force Terms

(a) Gravitational coupling re-expressed in tick units

{% raw %} \[ G_{\text{tick}} =\frac{\Delta x^{3}}{\tau^{2}\,M_{*}}, \qquad M_{*}\;\text{≡ effective mass scale.} \] {% endraw %}

Here G is not fundamental; it descends from lattice geometry once τ and Δx are fixed.

(b) Confinement string tension

{% raw %} \[ V(r)=\sigma\,r, \qquad \boxed{\; \sigma=\frac{2\pi\,E_{0}}{\Delta x} =\frac{2\pi\times6.892\,779\,493\;\text{meV}} {0.179\,875\,474\,8\;\text{mm}}\;} \] {% endraw %}

Linear growth appears because each tick adds a fixed energy quantum while elongating the flux tube by one spatial step Δx.

8 · Vacuum Energy & Cosmological Constant

(a) Time-tick formulation

{% raw %} \[ \rho_{\text{vac}} =\tfrac12\,E_{0}\, S_{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\, \Delta x^{-D_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \] {% endraw %}

(b) Frequency formulation

{% raw %} \[ \rho_{\text{vac}} =\frac{\eta\,S_{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}} {4\pi\,\xi^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}}\, f^{2}\, \bigl(c/f\bigr)^{2-D_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \] {% endraw %}

Both routes give the same ρvac ≈ 10⁻⁹ J m⁻³, implying Λ ≈ 10⁻⁵² m⁻².

9 · Discrete Friedmann Chain

(a) Finite-difference Hubble step

{% raw %} \[ \left( \frac{a_{N+1}-a_{N}}{\tau\,a_{N}} \right)^{2} =\frac{8\pi G_{\text{eff}}}{3}\, \rho_{b0}\left(\frac{a_{0}}{a_{N}}\right)^{3} -\frac{k_{\text{eff}}}{a_{N}^{2}} . \] {% endraw %}

(b) Scale-dependent G

{% raw %} \[ G_{\text{eff}}(r) =G\left(\frac{r}{r_{0}}\right)^{3-D_{\mathrm{eff}}} =G\left(\frac{r}{r_{0}}\right)^{0.333}. \] {% endraw %}

With keff = 0 and Λ ≈ 0 the iteration recovers a(t) ∝ t²⁄³ as τ → 0; the discrete-time correction lowers the effective cosmic age to teff ≈ 0.65 × 13.8 Gyr ≈ 9 Gyr.

10 · Tick-Induced Red-Shift

Standard relation 1 + z = a(t₀)/a(temit) becomes

{% raw %} \[ 1+z =\prod_{i=1}^{N}(1+\delta_{i}) \approx\exp\!\Bigl(\sum_{i=1}^{N}\delta_{i}\Bigr) \approx1+N\,\delta, \] {% endraw %}

where δ is the phase advance per tick along the photon path. For δ ≈ 10⁻¹² a red-shift z ≈ 1 accumulates after N ≈ 10¹² ticks (~190 Myr).
