Fundamental Tick and Time Unit We define nature's fundamental tick \( T_0 \) as the inverse of the frequency: \[ T_0 = \frac{1}{1.667 \times 10^{12}\,\text{Hz}} \approx 6.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{s}. \] This is the base time interval over which quantum transitions are computed. ## 2. 3D Full-Cycle Condition and Energy Quantum In each tick, the phase accumulated by an energy \( E \) is given by: \[ \phi = \frac{E\,T_0}{\hbar}. \] For a complete cycle (\( n = 1 \)), the phase must equal \( 2\pi \): \[ \frac{E\,T_0}{\hbar} = 2\pi. \] Thus, the energy quantum is: \[ E = \frac{2\pi\,\hbar}{T_0}. \] Using \(\hbar \approx 1.0546 \times 10^{-34}\,\text{J·s}\) and \( T_0 \approx 6.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{s} \), we obtain: \[ E \approx 6.89\,\text{meV} \quad \text{(in a full 3D Euclidean treatment)}. \] ## 3. Effective Fractal Scaling (2.667D) The AeonLoop model assumes that the effective geometry is not strictly 3D but exhibits a fractal nature with an effective dimension of about \(2.667\). This reduces the effective degrees of freedom. Detailed scaling arguments then reduce the energy quantum from the 3D value of approximately \(6.89\,\text{meV}\) to an effective value of roughly \(5\,\text{meV}\). Thus, while the full-cycle condition yields: \[ E_{\text{3D}} = \frac{2\pi\,\hbar}{T_0} \approx 6.89\,\text{meV}, \] after adjusting for the effective fractal dimension, we find: \[ E_{\text{eff}} \approx 5\,\text{meV}. \] ## 4. Discrete, Tick-Based Amplitude Calculation Conventional Feynman diagrams involve continuous integrals over an infinite range of intermediate states. In our model, every quantum process occurs in discrete ticks of length \( T_0 \). Only transitions that satisfy the full-cycle phase condition are allowed: \[ \frac{\Delta E\,T_0}{\hbar} = 2\pi\,n,\quad \text{with } n \in \mathbb{Z}^+. \] This restricts \( \Delta E \) to be integer multiples of the effective energy quantum (approximately \(5\,\text{meV}\)). Thus, the quantum amplitude for a process occurring over \( N \) ticks is constructed as a discrete sum over allowed transitions: \[ \mathcal{A} = \sum_{\{ n_i \}} \prod_{i=1}^{N} a(n_i), \] where each \( a(n_i) \) corresponds to the transition amplitude in the \( i \)-th tick, and non-compliant (non-integer phase) paths cancel out. ## 5.
