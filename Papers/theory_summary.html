Theory Summary

Enhanced Quantum Computing Architectures:

-------------------------------------------

• Our theory introduces a deterministic evolution using discrete time ticks (e.g., 0.6 ps) combined with a snap-back mechanism.

• This built-in error correction leads to robust nonlocal entanglement.

• As a result, qubits can be designed to be more stable and scalable, potentially operating at room temperature with less overhead.

Simplified Quantum Path Calculations:

---------------------------------------

• Instead of summing over an intractable number of paths (as in Feynman's path integrals), our approach selects only the 'useful' paths that reinforce coherence.

• This results in a more efficient computation method that streamlines the analysis of quantum processes.

New Insights into Fundamental Physics:

----------------------------------------

• Dark Energy: The theory reinterprets dark energy as an emergent property of the universe's coherence--decoherence cycles.

• Black Holes: Rather than singularities, black holes are viewed as regions where a snap-back mechanism confines light via perfect 3D phase closure, suggesting they may play an active role in cosmic evolution.

• Entanglement: The deterministic evolution, while precise, inherently supports nonlocal correlations, thereby explaining entangled states without invoking superluminal communication.

Improved Predictive Power and Experimental Testability:

---------------------------------------------------------

• The model yields clear, testable predictions regarding quantum coherence, entanglement, and phase dynamics.

• By quantizing time and enforcing phase closure, the theory opens new experimental avenues and may lead to the development of next-generation quantum devices.

Conceptual Clarity and Research Opportunities:

-----------------------------------------------

• The theory clearly distinguishes determinism from locality: deterministic evolution does not force local behavior.

• This redefinition fosters new research directions, allowing us to rethink not only quantum computing architectures but also fundamental questions in cosmology and quantum mechanics.

In summary, the theory provides:

• A more efficient computational framework by focusing on useful quantum paths.

• Robust, intrinsically error-correcting qubit designs.

• A unified view that deepens our understanding of dark energy, black holes, and nonlocal entanglement.

It challenges the traditional notion that determinism implies locality, instead proposing that a deterministic structure can underlie and even enhance nonlocal quantum interactions.
