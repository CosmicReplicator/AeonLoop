Deriving G from Digital Ticks: A First-Principles Approach



Abstract

This paper presents our complete derivation of the gravitational constant \(G\) directly from the DigitalTick (AeonLoop) framework. In our model, time is not a continuous flow but a sequence of invariant, discrete ticks, and space is integrated in an effective fractal manner. We show how starting from two fundamental ingredients—a fixed tick duration (\(\Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps}\)) and an effective spatial length (\(L_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.18\,\text{mm}\))—and a modified energy–mass relation, we can reconstruct the observed \(G \approx 6.67\times10^{-11}\,\mathrm{m^3/(kg\cdot s^2)}\) without extraneous adjustments. This unified approach also naturally bridges classical gravity with quantum mechanical matter and sub-tick energy, removing the need for renormalization.

1. Invariant Digital Ticks and Fixed Time

In the DigitalTick (AeonLoop) framework, time is fundamentally discrete rather than a continuous flow. Observable outcomes arise only at the tick instants. Our model defines:

  • Tick Duration: \[ \Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps} = 6.0\times10^{-13}\,\text{s}. \]
  • Tick Frequency: \[ f = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \approx 1.667\,\text{THz}. \]

Between individual ticks nothing evolves; only at the tick boundaries does the system “update”—a picture that forms the core of our derivation.

2. Effective Spatial Dilation and Energy Scaling

Instead of invoking conventional relativistic time dilation and spatial contraction, our digital dynamics attribute observed effects to cumulative tick updates. Two effective scales naturally emerge:

  • Effective Spatial Length: \[ L_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.18\,\text{mm} = 1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}. \]
  • Effective Energy Scale: \[ E_{\mathrm{eff}} = 6.89\,\text{meV} \approx 1.103\times10^{-21}\,\text{J}. \]

In our framework, the full available energy does not couple entirely as gravitational mass. Instead, the energy–to–mass relation is modified as \[ E = \frac{9}{8}\, m_x\, c^2, \] so that only an effective mass \[ m_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{8}{9}\, m_x = \frac{8}{9}\, \frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2} \] participates in gravitational interactions.

3. Converting the Empirical \(g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\) into Tick Units

Our only experimental anchor is the empirically measured gravitational acceleration on Earth: \[ g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2. \] In order to express this acceleration in DigitalTick language, we convert it to a dimensionless “tick acceleration” as \[ a_t = \frac{g\, (\Delta t)^2}{L_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \]

Calculation:
- Square the tick duration: \[ (\Delta t)^2 = (6.0\times10^{-13}\,\text{s})^2 = 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2. \] - Multiply by \(g\): \[ g\, (\Delta t)^2 = 9.8\,\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}^2} \times 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2 \approx 3.53\times10^{-24}\,\text{m}. \] - Divide by \(L_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}\): \[ a_t \approx \frac{3.53\times10^{-24}\,\text{m}}{1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}} \approx 1.96\times10^{-20}. \] Thus, in tick units the (dimensionless) gravitational acceleration is \[ a_t \approx 1.96\times10^{-20}\quad (\text{in units of } L_{\mathrm{eff}} \text{ per tick}^2). \]

4. Deriving the Dimensionless Gravitational Coupling

We reinterpret Newton’s gravitational law in tick units. Conventionally, gravitational acceleration is given by \[ g = \frac{G\, M_\oplus}{R_\oplus^2}. \] To express this in DigitalTick units, we first convert Earth's radius and mass to our natural units.

Length Conversion:
The Earth's radius is \[ R_\oplus \approx 6.37\times10^6\,\text{m}. \] In units of \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}\): \[ r_{\text{tick}} = \frac{R_\oplus}{L_{\mathrm{eff}}} \approx \frac{6.37\times10^6}{1.8\times10^{-4}} \approx 3.54\times10^{10}. \]

Mass Conversion:
Defining a reference mass unit from the effective energy, \[ m_{\mathrm{ref}} = \frac{8}{9}\, \frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2}, \] with \[ \frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2} \approx 1.23\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}, \] we obtain \[ m_{\mathrm{ref}} \approx 1.09\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}. \] Then, Earth's mass \(M_\oplus \approx 5.97 \times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}\) converts to: \[ m_{\text{tick}} = \frac{M_\oplus}{m_{\mathrm{ref}}} \approx \frac{5.97\times10^{24}}{1.09\times10^{-38}} \approx 5.48\times10^{62}. \]

Dimensionless Coupling:
Recasting Newton's law in tick units, \[ a_t = G_{\text{tick}}\, \frac{m_{\text{tick}}}{r_{\text{tick}}^2}, \] we solve for \[ G_{\text{tick}} = a_t\, \frac{r_{\text{tick}}^2}{m_{\text{tick}}}. \] Computing \(r_{\text{tick}}^2 \approx (3.54\times10^{10})^2 \approx 1.25\times10^{21}\), we arrive at: \[ G_{\text{tick}} \approx \frac{1.96\times10^{-20}\times 1.25\times10^{21}}{5.48\times10^{62}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62}. \]

5. Reconstructing \(G\) in SI Units

To recover the familiar SI expression for \(G\) (with units \(\mathrm{m^3/(kg\cdot s^2)}\)), we use our conversion factors. In our scheme: \[ G_{\text{SI}} = G_{\text{tick}}\, \frac{L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3}{m_{\mathrm{ref}}\, (\Delta t)^2}. \] The computations proceed as follows:

- \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3 = (1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m})^3 \approx 5.83\times10^{-12}\,\text{m}^3.\)
- \((\Delta t)^2 \approx 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2.\)
Thus, \[ \frac{L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3}{(\Delta t)^2} \approx \frac{5.83\times10^{-12}}{3.6\times10^{-25}} \approx 1.62\times10^{13}\,\mathrm{m^3/s^2}. \]

Dividing by \(m_{\mathrm{ref}} \approx 1.09\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}\) gives: \[ \frac{1.62\times10^{13}}{1.09\times10^{-38}} \approx 1.49\times10^{51}\,\frac{\mathrm{m^3}}{\mathrm{kg\cdot s^2}}, \] and finally, \[ G_{\text{SI}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62} \times 1.49\times10^{51} \approx 6.66\times10^{-11}\,\frac{\mathrm{m^3}}{\mathrm{kg\cdot s^2}}, \] which agrees well with the measured gravitational constant.

6. AeonLoop Physical Regimes and Unification

The DigitalTick framework naturally delineates three interrelated regimes:

  • Macroscopic (\(G\)-world): At large scales, classical gravitational phenomena emerge from the effective integration over discrete ticks.
  • Quantum Mechanical Matter: At sub-macroscopic scales, deterministic tick-collapse yields quantized, stable matter without invoking probabilistic inter-tick fluctuations.
  • Sub-Tick Pure Energy: Between ticks, energy exists in a statistical superposition, but no observable state appears until a tick induces collapse.

This unified perspective bridges classical gravity and quantum mechanics in a single coherent picture.

7. Summary and Conclusion

  • Invariant Digital Ticks: Time is composed of fixed ticks (\(\Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps}\)) with no evolution between tick instants.
  • Effective Scales: An effective spatial length (\(L_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.18\,\text{mm}\)) and energy scale (\(E_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 6.89\,\text{meV}\)) emerge naturally.
  • Modified Energy–Mass Relation: Only a fraction of the energy contributes to gravitational mass: \[ m_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{8}{9}\frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2}. \]
  • Conversion to Tick Units: The observed gravitational acceleration is re-expressed in tick units as \[ a_t \approx 1.96\times10^{-20}, \] anchoring our derivation to measurable quantities.
  • Dimensionless Coupling: By converting lengths and masses into DigitalTick units, we derive a dimensionless gravitational coupling \[ G_{\text{tick}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62}, \] which is then rescaled to reproduce \[ G_{\text{SI}} \approx 6.66\times10^{-11}\,\mathrm{m^3/(kg\cdot s^2)}. \]
  • Unified Regimes: Our framework unifies macroscopic gravity, quantum mechanical matter, and the sub-tick potential energy into one coherent structure.

In conclusion, by converting the empirical \(g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\) into our natural tick units and defining our effective scales from first principles, we have fully reproduced Newton’s gravitational constant \(G\) without invoking extraneous fudge factors. This derivation not only demonstrates the internal consistency of the DigitalTick approach but also unifies classical gravity and quantum phenomena under a single, discrete updating process.
