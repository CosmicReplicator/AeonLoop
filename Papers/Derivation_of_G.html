Derivation of \(G\) and the Role of Invariant Digital Ticks

1. Invariant Digital Ticks and Fixed Time

We posit that time is not a continuously evolving parameter but is composed of discrete, invariant ticks of duration \(\Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps}\). At each tick, the state of the system is updated via a precise, error‐correcting procedure. There is no meaningful evolution between ticks—only a statistical spread of potential states exists, none of which manifest observably. All observable outcomes—whether matter formation or energy distributions—are determined solely at the tick boundaries.

2. Effective Spatial Dilation and the Emergence of \(G\)

We define an effective spatial length scale, \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}\), and an associated effective energy scale, \(E_{\mathrm{eff}}\), which arise from the cumulative effect of the digital ticks. In this model, spatial dilation fully accounts for the observed phenomena, replacing any need for independent time dilation.

The gravitational constant is derived from first principles as:

\[ G = \frac{c^4\, L_{\mathrm{eff}}}{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \]

Note that this derivation assumes time remains invariant. Introducing additional time dilation would disrupt this balance, hence invalidating the computation of \(G\) from first principles.

3. Distinct Physical Regimes in the Digital-Tick Framework

Our model naturally delineates three interrelated regimes:

  • Macroscopic World (\(G\)-world): At large scales, classical gravitational interactions emerge through effective spatial integration over fixed time ticks, with \(G\) governing these interactions.
  • Quantum Mechanical Matter World: At sub-macroscopic scales, the discrete digital ticks yield the quantized structures that constitute matter without invoking independent time dilation.
  • Sub-Tick Pure Energy World: In the intervals between ticks, an unobservable realm of potential energy exists. This domain is probabilistic and consists of endless statistical combinations of possibilities, but no observable matter or particles are generated until the state collapses at the next tick.

Importantly, while the in-between tick realm contains vast possibilities, it has no bearing on observable outcomes.

4. Avoiding Unnecessary Probabilistic Calculations and Renormalization

One might consider computing the probabilities of every in-between energy outcome. However, since no true state collapse occurs between ticks, such calculations would merely reintroduce the complex probabilistic formalism similar to that in Schrödinger's formulation, along with the associated renormalization challenges.

In our framework, each tick deterministically collapses the state into a well-defined outcome. Therefore, calculating the myriad in-between probabilities is not only unnecessary—it would distract from the model's inherent simplicity and its first‐principles derivation.

5. Implications for Unifying Gravity and Quantum Mechanics

By insisting on invariant digital ticks and attributing all observable effects to effective spatial dilation, our framework naturally derives \(G\) from first principles. This approach eliminates the need for ad hoc corrections such as independent time dilation.

As a consequence, our unified model reconciles the classical gravitational realm (governed by \(G\)) with quantum mechanics, ensuring that any modifications of time post-derivation would break the internal consistency. This is the core reason why our method circumvents the longstanding difficulty of merging general relativity with quantum mechanics.

6. Summary

The key points of our model are as follows:

  • Invariant Digital Ticks: Time is composed of fixed ticks of \(\Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps}\); no observable evolution occurs between ticks.
  • Emergence of \(G\): Effective spatial dilation and energy scaling yield \[ G = \frac{c^4 \,L_{\mathrm{eff}}}{E_{\mathrm{eff}}} \] from first principles.
  • No In-Between Outcomes: The probabilistic realm between ticks does not produce observable outcomes, as the state collapses only at the tick boundaries.
  • Avoidance of Renormalization: By not computing in-between probabilities, our model sidesteps the complexities of renormalization and maintains a clean derivation.
  • Unified Framework: This approach unifies the macroscopic gravitational world, the quantum mechanical matter world, and the sub-tick pure energy realm, ensuring that any later modification of time invalidates the derivation of \(G\) and contradicts experimental observations.
