1. Invariant Digital Ticks and Fixed Time


In the DigitalTick (AeonLoop) framework, time is not a continuous flow but a sequence of invariant, discrete ticks. In our model:

- The tick duration is defined as \(\Delta t = 0.6\,\text{ps} = 6.0\times10^{-13}\,\text{s}\),
- Corresponding to a tick frequency of \(f = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \approx 1.667\,\text{THz}\).

No physical evolution occurs between tick boundaries; observable outcomes are recorded only at the tick instants.

2. Effective Spatial Dilation and Energy Scaling


Instead of invoking relativistic time dilation, our approach attributes all observable effects to the cumulative effect of digital ticks. Two effective scales emerge:

- An effective spatial length, \(L_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.18\,\text{mm} = 1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}\),
- And an effective energy scale, \(E_{\mathrm{eff}} = 6.89\,\text{meV} \approx 1.103\times10^{-21}\,\text{J}\).

These scales arise naturally from the tick counting process. Importantly, our digital dynamics determine that the full energy does not collapse entirely into gravitational mass: instead, the energy–to–mass relation is modified as \[ E = \frac{9}{8}\, m_x\, c^2, \] so that only an effective mass \[ m_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{8}{9}\, m_x = \frac{8}{9}\,\frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2} \] contributes to gravity.

3. Converting the Empirical \(g=9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\) into Tick Units


The sole empirical anchor in our derivation is the observed gravitational acceleration on Earth: \[ g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2. \] To express this in our DigitalTick language we convert it into a dimensionless “tick acceleration” by writing: \[ a_t = \frac{g\, (\Delta t)^2}{L_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \] Calculation:
- First, square the tick duration: \[ (\Delta t)^2 = (6.0\times10^{-13}\,\text{s})^2 = 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2. \] - Multiply by \(g\): \[ g\, (\Delta t)^2 = 9.8\,\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}^2}\times 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2 \approx 3.53\times10^{-24}\,\text{m}. \] - Divide by \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}=1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}\): \[ a_t \approx \frac{3.53\times10^{-24}\,\text{m}}{1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m}} \approx 1.96\times10^{-20}. \]
Thus, in tick units the (dimensionless) gravitational acceleration is \[ a_t \approx 1.96\times10^{-20}\quad \text{(in units of } L_{\mathrm{eff}}\text{ per tick}^2\text{)}. \]

4. Deriving the Dimensionless Gravitational Coupling


We now reinterpret Newton’s gravitational law in terms of tick units. In conventional SI units the acceleration due to gravity is given by: \[ g = \frac{G\, M_\oplus}{R_\oplus^2}. \] In our scheme, we first express lengths and masses in natural DigitalTick units. Length Conversion:
The Earth’s radius in SI is \[ R_\oplus \approx 6.37\times10^6\,\text{m}. \] Expressed in units of \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}\): \[ r_{\text{tick}} = \frac{R_\oplus}{L_{\mathrm{eff}}} \approx \frac{6.37\times10^6}{1.8\times10^{-4}} \approx 3.54\times10^{10}. \] Mass Conversion:
We define a reference mass unit from the effective energy: \[ m_{\mathrm{ref}} = \frac{8}{9}\,\frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2}. \] With \(E_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 1.103\times10^{-21}\,\text{J}\) and \(c^2\approx9.0\times10^{16}\,\text{m}^2/\text{s}^2\), \[ \frac{E_{\mathrm{eff}}}{c^2} \approx 1.23\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}, \] and so: \[ m_{\mathrm{ref}} \approx 1.09\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}. \] The Earth’s mass \(M_\oplus \approx 5.97 \times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}\) then becomes: \[ m_{\text{tick}} = \frac{M_\oplus}{m_{\mathrm{ref}}} \approx \frac{5.97\times10^{24}}{1.09\times10^{-38}} \approx 5.48\times10^{62}. \] Dimensionless Coupling:
Rewriting Newton’s law in tick units, we assume a relation of the form: \[ a_t = G_{\text{tick}}\, \frac{m_{\text{tick}}}{r_{\text{tick}}^2}. \] Solving for \(G_{\text{tick}}\): \[ G_{\text{tick}} = a_t\, \frac{r_{\text{tick}}^2}{m_{\text{tick}}}. \] With \[ r_{\text{tick}}^2 \approx (3.54\times10^{10})^2 \approx 1.25\times10^{21}, \] we compute: \[ G_{\text{tick}} \approx \frac{1.96\times10^{-20}\times 1.25\times10^{21}}{5.48\times10^{62}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62}. \]

5. Reconstructing \(G\) in SI Units


To recover \(G\) in SI units (with dimensions \(\mathrm{m^3/(kg\cdot s^2)}\)), we use our DigitalTick conversion factors. In our scheme: \[ G_{\text{SI}} = G_{\text{tick}}\, \frac{L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3}{m_{\mathrm{ref}}\, (\Delta t)^2}. \] Calculations: - \(L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3 = (1.8\times10^{-4}\,\text{m})^3 \approx 5.83\times10^{-12}\,\text{m}^3.\)
- \((\Delta t)^2 = (6.0\times10^{-13}\,\text{s})^2 \approx 3.6\times10^{-25}\,\text{s}^2.\)
Thus, \[ \frac{L_{\mathrm{eff}}^3}{(\Delta t)^2} \approx \frac{5.83\times10^{-12}}{3.6\times10^{-25}} \approx 1.62\times10^{13}\,\mathrm{m^3/s^2}. \] Dividing by \(m_{\mathrm{ref}} \approx 1.09\times10^{-38}\,\text{kg}\) yields: \[ \frac{1.62\times10^{13}}{1.09\times10^{-38}} \approx 1.49\times10^{51}\,\frac{\mathrm{m^3}}{\mathrm{kg\cdot s^2}}. \] Finally, multiplying by \(G_{\text{tick}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62}\), we obtain: \[ G_{\text{SI}} \approx 4.47\times10^{-62} \times 1.49\times10^{51} \approx 6.66\times10^{-11}\,\frac{\mathrm{m^3}}{\mathrm{kg\cdot s^2}}, \] which matches the observed gravitational constant.

6. AeonLoop Physical Regimes and Unification


The DigitalTick framework distinguishes three natural regimes:
  • Macroscopic (\(G\)-world): At large scales, classical gravitational phenomena emerge via effective spatial integration over discrete ticks.
  • Quantum Mechanical Matter: At sub-macroscopic scales, deterministic tick-collapse yields quantized, stable matter without invoking probabilistic inter-tick processes.
  • Sub-Tick Pure Energy: Between ticks, potential energy exists in a statistical superposition, but no observable state appears until a tick induces collapse.
This unified view naturally bridges classical gravity and quantum mechanics within one coherent DigitalTick framework.

  • Unified Regimes: Macroscopic gravitational phenomena, quantum mechanical matter, and sub-tick potential energy are unified by the DigitalTick dynamics.
