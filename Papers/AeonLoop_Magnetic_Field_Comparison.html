Digital-Tick Model: Vacuum Magnetic Field Comparison

This document presents a stress test of the digital-tick model by calculating the vacuum magnetic field \(B_{\text{rms}}\) without using divergent integrals or arbitrary renormalization—relying solely on the fundamental digital-tick parameters.

Fundamental Digital-Tick Parameters:

  • Tick Time \(\Delta t\): \(0.6\,\text{ps}\)
  • Tick Frequency \(f_0\): \(\sim1.667\,\text{THz}\) (with \(f_0 = \frac{1}{\Delta t}\))
  • Characteristic Length \(L\): \(L = \frac{c}{f_0} \approx 0.18\,\text{mm}\) (with \(c \approx 3\times10^{8}\,\text{m/s}\))
  • Energy per Tick \(E\): \(E = h\cdot f_0 \approx 6.9\,\text{meV}\) (with \(h \approx 6.626\times10^{-34}\,\text{Js}\))
  • Effective Fractal Dimension \(D_{\text{eff}}\): \(2.667\)

Finite Vacuum Energy Density:

The digital-tick model yields a renormalization‑free vacuum energy density:

\(\displaystyle \rho_{\text{vac}} = \frac{1}{2}\,\hbar\,f_0 \left[\frac{S_{D_{\text{eff}}}}{(2\pi)^{D_{\text{eff}}}}\right] \left[\frac{2\pi\,f_0}{c}\right]^{D_{\text{eff}}}\)

where:

  • \(\hbar\) is the reduced Planck constant \(\bigl(h/(2\pi)\bigr)\)
  • \(S_{D_{\text{eff}}} = \frac{2\pi^{(D_{\text{eff}}/2)}}{\Gamma(D_{\text{eff}}/2)}\) is the effective unit "surface area"

Using the parameters above, a numerical evaluation gives:

\(\rho_{\text{vac}} \approx 8.80\times10^{-12}\,\text{J/m}^3\)

Vacuum Magnetic Field Estimate:

Assuming that the electromagnetic vacuum energy is partitioned equally between electric and magnetic fields, the magnetic field energy density is defined by:

\(\displaystyle u_B = \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0}\)

Equating \(u_B \approx \rho_{\text{vac}}\) leads to an estimate for the rms vacuum magnetic field:

\(\displaystyle B_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\mu_0\,\rho_{\text{vac}}}\)

with \(\mu_0 = 4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\text{H/m}\). The calculation yields:

\(B_{\text{rms}} \approx 3.33\times10^{-9}\,\text{T}\)

Comparison with Conventional Expectations:

Standard QED treatments—when regulated with a physical cutoff—predict a vacuum magnetic field amplitude on the order of a few nanotesla (i.e., \(1\,\text{nT} = 1\times10^{-9}\,\text{T}\)). Our digital-tick model produces a value in the same ballpark, confirming that by imposing an invariant tick, the divergences are eliminated and a finite, physically meaningful result is obtained.

Summary:

  • Digital-tick parameters yield a finite vacuum energy density, \(\rho_{\text{vac}}\), without ad hoc renormalization.
  • This, in turn, gives a finite vacuum magnetic field: \(B_{\text{rms}} \approx 3.33\times10^{-9}\,\text{T}\).
  • The magnitude of the vacuum magnetic field (∼ a few \(\times10^{-9}\) to \(10^{-8}\,\text{T}\)) is comparable with conventional (cutoff-regulated) estimates, reinforcing the validity of the digital-tick approach.
