From Digital Ticks to Emergent Forces — A Cascade of Resonant Steps

Abstract

We start from an invariant tick τ ≈ 0.600 ps (f ≈ 1.667 THz, E₀ ≈ 6.892 779 493 meV). A single amplifier \(Q\) ≈ 409.78 lifts that seed by five resonant steps to the electroweak scale \(E_5\) ≈ 80 GeV; step 4 lands on the QCD confinement scale (~ 195 MeV). Continuing to steps 6 – 8 predicts ~ 32.6 TeV, 13.4 PeV, and 5.47 EeV, hinting that the hierarchy of forces might be an arithmetic consequence of one digital tick.

--- ## 1 Introduction

The strong/weak hierarchy is usually framed as a continuous renormalisation-group flow. Here we explore a digital-tick alternative: the universe advances in equal quanta τ. Earlier work fixed \(D_{\text{eff}}=\tfrac83\) via a seven-segment phase-lock; now we push the same logic up the energy ladder.

--- ## 4 Forces from the ladder Step 4 reproduces ΛQCD, step 5 the W/Z mass window, and steps 6–8 coincide with observed breaks in the cosmic-ray spectrum. Hence the weak interaction may emerge one rung above the strong scale, not from an unrelated cutoff. --- ## 5 Discussion A single Q reproduces two pillars of the Standard Model and projects smoothly into the ultra-high-energy regime with no extra knobs. Because Q is fixed once \(E_5\) is chosen, the model avoids fine-tuning in the usual sense. --- ## 6 Conclusions & next questions 1.
