Fractal Torsion: The fundamental “twist” or torsion repeats self-similarly over all scales. Both microscopic dynamics (e.g., particle spin) and macroscopic structures (e.g., galactic rotations) reflect this unified, fractal architecture.

0.667D Spin Network: The effective dimensionality is not the usual 3D but a reduced, fractional one (0.667D) that arises from the digital‐tick dynamics. This enforces the idea that the underlying “space” in the model is discretized—a partially filled arena defined by intrinsic ticking.

RH Dominance: The coherent spinning disc rotates in a right-handed (RH) manner. This intrinsic chirality sets a natural bias, ensuring that stable, phase‐corrected states conform to RH configurations.

This parameter serves as a bridge between the micro‐dynamics of digital ticks and the macroscopic observables:

- The discrete update (tick) process introduces subtle phase errors that are cumulatively corrected over a full (7‐tick) cycle.

- \( \gamma_B \) quantifies the net effect of these phase corrections, effectively setting the “twist intensity” required for viable, stable configurations.

- With \( \gamma_B = 0.3\pi \), the model translates the digital tick sequence into a predictable scaling law that influences energy levels and emergent mass relations.

From the underlying digital‐tick dynamics and chiral bias emerge several key phenomena:

– LH states are unstable and decay following a precise scaling law.
– LH matter cannot spontaneously flip its chirality into a stable RH state; its persistence requires being captured by an existing RH scaffold.
