1. Discrete Update of the Electromagnetic Potential

In our model, we assume that at each digital tick---every Δt = 0.6 ps---the electromagnetic four--potential, denoted by A_mu(t, x), is updated by a rule of the form:\ \ A_mu(t + Δt, x) = A_mu(t, x) + Δt * A_mu_dot(t, x) + O((Δt)\^2),\ \ where A_mu_dot(t, x) is determined by the dynamics (ultimately, by an underlying error--correction rule). This is analogous to a finite difference approximation:\ \ A_mu_dot(t, x) ≈ (A_mu(t + Δt, x) -- A_mu(t, x)) / Δt.

2. Constructing the Field Tensor from Finite Differences

Recall that the electromagnetic field tensor is defined through the potential as:\ \ F_{mu nu} = ∂_mu A_nu - ∂_nu A_mu.\ \ In our discrete picture, the partial derivatives are approximated by finite differences. For example, for the time derivative:\ \ ∂_t A_nu(t, x) ≈ (A_nu(t + Δt, x) - A_nu(t, x)) / Δt.\ \ Similarly, spatial derivatives are replaced by finite differences over the appropriate spatial intervals. In the limit as Δt (and the spatial step Δx) → 0, the finite differences approach the standard derivatives, recovering the familiar definition of F_{mu nu}.

3. Discrete Version of the Lagrangian and Euler--Lagrange Equations

For electromagnetism, the classical Lagrangian density is given by:\ \ L = -1/4 F_{mu nu} F\^{mu nu}.\ \ In our discrete framework, we formulate a lattice version of this Lagrangian, replacing the derivatives with finite differences. Defining an action by summing over the nodes and digital ticks (t, t+Δt, t+2Δt, ...), the discrete Euler--Lagrange equations take the form:\ \ (A_mu(t + Δt, x) - A_mu(t, x)) / Δt = ∂L/∂(∂_t A\^mu),\ \ and in the continuum limit (Δt → 0), we recover the standard Euler--Lagrange equations. Variation of the continuum action yields:\ \ ∂_mu F\^{mu nu} = J\^nu,\ \ which are precisely Maxwell's equations (with source terms, where J\^nu is the four--current).
